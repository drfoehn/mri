1++ High quality meta-analyses, systematic reviews of RCTs, or RCTs with a very low risk of bias High
1+ Well-conducted meta-analyses, systematic reviews of RCTs, or RCTs with a low risk of bias High
1- Meta-analyses, systematic reviews of RCTs, or RCTs with a high risk of bias Moderate
2++ High quality systematic reviews of case-control or cohort studies; high quality case-control or cohort studies with a very low risk of confounding or bias and a high probability that the relationship is causal Moderate
2+ Well-conducted case-control or cohort studies with a low risk of confounding or bias and a moderate probability that the relationship is causal Moderate
2- Case-control or cohort studies with a high risk of confounding or bias and a significant risk that the relationship is not causal Low
3 Non-analytic studies (for example, case reports, case series) Very Low
4 Expert opinion Very Low
GPP Good Practice Point - recommended best practice based on the clinical experience of the guideline development group Best Practice
